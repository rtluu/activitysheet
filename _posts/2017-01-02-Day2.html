While working with Osebo on Stash today, we decided to dig into the true problem that we're attempting to solve with Stash. It started with a question. Why do people collect things?

"Collecting is a basic human instinct; a survival advantage amplified by eons of natural selection. Those of our ancient ancestors who managed to accumulate scarce objects may have been more prone to survive long enough to bear offspring." (source)

If you think about it, everyone collects something. Baseball cards, money, pictures, letters from loved ones, sneakers... something. Our motivations to collect stem from a few common reasons including:

  • Knowledge and learning
  • Relaxation and stress reduction
  • Personal pleasure
  • Social interaction with fellow collectors and others
  • Competitive challenge
  • Recognition by fellow collectors and perhaps even non-collectors
  • The desire to control, possess and bring order to part of the world
  • Nostalgia and/or a connection to history
  • Accumulation and diversification of wealth

So how does this apply to Stash? We feel like collecting content on the web is difficult, unappealing, and hidden from others who can find value in it.

Right now, to collect webpages, images, videos, etc., people either share links on chat platforms where they get lost in conversation, they create bookmarks that get cluttered, or they have to create their own websites that are tedious to build/maintain.

Our goal is to make it easire for people to create, share, and discover collections.
